# Dr. Tyler Nelson / Camp 4 Human Performance — Methodology Notes

Researched 2026-05-28. Tyler Nelson DC, MS, CSCS — co-founder of C4HP, Tindeq advisor, climbing-medicine specialist. Distinctive stance: tendon-and-rehab-first, autoregulated isometrics, skeptical of dogmatic max-hang prescriptions.

## Core philosophy

- **Isometrics specifically for climbers** because they're specific to gripping, easy to execute correctly without supervision, and load tendons without joint stress.
- **Autoregulation > fixed % loads.** Use a Tindeq Progressor to read peak force and prescribe relative to today's capacity, not last cycle's number.
- **24-hour pain response is the primary biological feedback signal.** If pain is up 24 h after a session, the tendon is not adapting — back off intensity or volume.
- **Loaded position matters more than load.** Open-hand and half-crimp at 25–30 mm are tendon-friendly; full crimp is reserved for sport-specific peaking.

## Key protocols

### 1. Max-strength testing on Tindeq

- 2–4 second maximal pull, 2–3 reps per hand with full rest, both hands separately.
- Peak force = current 1RM analog. Drives all relative-intensity prescriptions for the next block.

### 2. Density Hangs (his hallmark tendon-and-MTU protocol)

- **Hold:** 20–40 s per rep
- **Intensity:** 55–85% MVC-7, **~75% is the sweet spot**
- **Sets:** 4–9 per session (8 is common target)
- **Reps per set:** 2–3 to muscular failure
- **Rest between reps within a set:** 10–20 s (2:1 hang:rest ratio)
- **Rest between sets:** 3–5 min
- **Edge:** 20–35 mm (larger to protect pulleys)
- **Grips:** half crimp, open hand, slopers, even pinch
- **When:** off-season / base block; tendon-restoration phases

### 3. Submaximal Repeaters (his endurance/density variation)

- 5 s on / 2–3 s off, or 7 s on / 3 s off × 15 reps
- 40–50% intensity
- Set to failure or grip slip
- Volume regression across sets is expected (e.g., 10 / 8 / 6 reps)

### 4. "Force tendons" type heavy isometric work

- 2–4 s contractions at 80–90% MVC on the Tindeq
- 4–6 sets, 2–3 min rest
- Used when chasing peak strength expressed isometrically
- This is the **80% MVC standard Martin referenced** — it's a heavy short-duration overcoming-isometric block, distinct from his density work.

## Pulley-injury rehab principles (relevant to Martin's healed left A2)

- Load in **open hand** first — bodyweight is usually painless even early.
- Progress to **half-crimp** only when 24 h pain is flat and load tolerance is solid.
- **Full crimp is the last position to reintroduce.**
- Hand asymmetry is corrected with **unilateral isometric work on the weaker side** at submaximal loads. Match volume (set count) on the stronger side at the same relative %, not the same absolute load — otherwise the gap stays open.
- Consider **Blood-Flow-Restriction (BFR)** training at low loads (20–30% 1RM) for hypertrophy without high tissue stress — Nelson is a major advocate.

## Where Nelson diverges from Lattice / Eva López

- Skeptical of "% bodyweight" added-load max hangs as a routine prescription. Prefers % MVC measured per session on a Tindeq, because daily capacity varies.
- More tendon-and-tissue oriented; less performance-output oriented.
- Comfortable with much longer hold durations (20–40 s) than Lattice's 5–10 s max-hang dogma.
- Treats grip-position rotation (open / half / full / sloper / pinch) as a structural-health requirement, not an option.

## Key takeaways for Martin

1. **Use the Tindeq to measure both peak force and asymmetry every 4 weeks.** Test both hands separately. Drive intensity off the weaker hand's MVC when training balance.
2. **The 80% MVC standard you mentioned = Nelson's heavy short-duration isometric protocol.** Prescription: 4–6 sets of 2–4 s pulls at 80% MVC, 2–3 min rest, 2× per week. Pair this with one density-hang day if there's room.
3. **Open the cycle (Phase 1) with Density Hangs at 75% MVC on a 25–30 mm edge** to rebuild tissue capacity before stacking high-intensity max work. This is the safest re-entry given the healed A2 and a 6-week climbing trip likely leaving fatigued tendons.
4. **Pulley asymmetry fix:** train left hand first while fresh, match set count not load on right, and re-test every 4 weeks. Expect the gap to close in 8–12 weeks.
5. **24 h rule:** if next-morning soreness in the left middle finger pulley region exceeds mild stiffness, that session was too much. Use this as the autoregulation signal, not RPE alone.
