# Hooper's Beta — Methodology Notes

Researched 2026-05-28. Dr. Jason Hooper, PT, DPT, OCS, SCS — physical-therapist-turned-climbing-content-creator. The generalist's reference: warm-ups, antagonist work, rehab, mobility, return-to-sport.

## Core stance

- "Supplement climbing, don't replace it." Strength work is the side dish.
- Prehab is non-negotiable: a few minutes of structured warm-up beats an hour of stretching after the fact.
- Rehab and training are on the same continuum — load progression respects tissue tolerance.

## The standard warm-up (3-step, ~10 min)

1. **Tendon gliding** — sequenced finger curls that move the FDP and FDS independently through their full range. Wakes up flexor tendons before any pulling.
2. **Bilateral external rotation with scapular retraction** (band) — primes infraspinatus, teres minor, rhomboids. Climbers' chronically internally-rotated shoulders need this.
3. **Recruitment pulls** — progressive hangboard or edge pulls from very low to near-max effort over 5–8 sets, 3 min rest, ramping nerve conduction and pulley tolerance.

Single theraband + a hangboard edge is the full toolkit.

## Minimalist strength routine (post-climbing)

Use **2× per week** if experienced (Hooper's recommendation; max 3× if recovery allows; 1× when starting).

### Pre-climb (warm-up + activation)
- Recruitment pulls — 5–8 sets, 3 min rest
- No Moneys (band external rotation) — 8–12 reps × up to 3 sets
- Stick dislocates — 30–60 s × up to 3 sets

### Post-climb supersets (1–2 RIR — reps in reserve)
**Superset 1 — vertical pull/push (primary):**
- Pull-ups 5–8 reps
- Dips 5–8 reps
- 3 rounds, 3–5 min rest

**Superset 2 — horizontal pull/push (optional):**
- Rows 5–8 reps
- Bench press 5–8 reps
- 3 rounds, 3–5 min rest

For Martin: this routine **slots cleanly into a pull-push-rest split** and the loaded pull-up at 1–2 RIR can be his progression vehicle for the 55 kg-added goal.

## Pulley rehab (the A2 manual)

Hooper has the most-recommended free A2 pulley protocol in the climbing community. Stages:

1. **Acute (week 0–2):** ice/compression, tendon glides, no climbing
2. **Sub-acute (2–6):** open-hand low-load isometrics, easy climbing on jugs, H-tape if needed
3. **Loading (6–12):** progressive half-crimp loading, hangboard at low %, no full crimp
4. **Return to climbing (12–16+):** all positions reintroduced, asymmetry monitored
5. **Performance:** full training resumed when load tolerance matches contralateral side

For Martin's healed A2: he's clearly past stage 4–5. The remaining job is **closing the asymmetry** while staying within the 24 h pain rule (this aligns perfectly with Nelson's approach).

## Mobility and ACL/knee work

Hooper has a "functional mobility" follow-along covering hip, shoulder, knee, spine in one routine. He doesn't publish an ACL-specific climbing return-to-sport doc, but the relevant principles repeat in his videos:

- Mobility is daily and short, not occasional and long.
- For the surgical knee at 2+ years post-op: progressive end-range loading (deep-squat assisted, then unsupported, eventually loaded), tibial-rotation drills, and tolerance-led progression. No fixed timeline — milestones (pain-free deep squat BW, 5k pain-free run, single-leg balance) determine progression.
- His Recovery Blueprint product targets exactly this kind of "return confidently" framing for adult athletes.

## Antagonist + core

- Push : pull ratio target ~1:1 in volume terms, especially for over-40 climbers whose shoulder health depends on it.
- Core: front-loaded carries, hollow holds, anti-rotation (Pallof) > sit-ups.
- 2× per week is plenty if integrated into climbing days as supersets.

## Running and climbing

Hooper has not published a strict interference-effect protocol, but the consistent advice in his content:

- Easy-pace running is compatible with climbing strength work if kept short (≤30–45 min) and on rest or push days.
- Avoid high-intensity intervals on consecutive days with finger training — both stress connective tissue.
- For Martin's recent return-to-running (5 k at 8:30/km, 2 yr post-ACL): keep it conversational pace, 2–3× per week, build duration before pace.

## Key takeaways for Martin

1. **Adopt his 3-step warm-up verbatim before every finger session.** It is the cheapest injury insurance in the sport.
2. **The minimalist strength routine maps directly onto a pull-push-rest week** — Pull-Up + Dips superset on the pull day, Row + Bench on the push day. The 5–8 reps × 3 sets at 1–2 RIR is the right rep range for moving 18 kg → 30 kg added pull-up across 12 weeks.
3. **Mobility is daily, short, integrated.** 10–15 min before climbing or in evenings, not standalone 60-min sessions. Hip + hamstring + ankle bias for the deep-squat goal.
4. **Knee:** keep running at conversational pace until you can hold 5 km at sub-8:00/km pain-free; only then consider tempo or longer runs. Single-leg work (split squats, step-ups) is the missing piece for ACL maturity at 2+ years.
5. **Push-pull volume balance** — over the 12 weeks, dips/bench work should keep pace with weighted-pull volume to protect the shoulders into your 40s.
