# Periodization for Climbing — Methodology Notes

Researched 2026-05-28. Comparative notes across Lattice, Eric Hörst, Steve Bechtel, and current peer-reviewed work. Aimed at a 12-week (3 × 4-week mesocycle) plan for a 43-year-old sport climber.

## Vocabulary

- **Macrocycle** — the full 6–12 month arc, usually built around an outdoor performance window.
- **Mesocycle** — a 3–6 week block with a single dominant adaptation target (e.g., max strength, power endurance). 4 weeks is the climbing default.
- **Microcycle** — one week, the repeating training structure.
- **Deload** — reduced-load week (typically every 4th) for fatigue clearance and supercompensation.

## Three competing periodization models

### 1. Linear periodization (classic — Hörst's 4-3-2-1)

Eric Hörst's published 10-week cycle:
- **4 weeks** — endurance & tactics (volume, technique, base aerobic capacity)
- **3 weeks** — strength & power (hangboard, weighted pulls, bouldering limit)
- **2 weeks** — anaerobic endurance / power endurance (route intervals, repeaters)
- **1 week** — peak / rest

Strength: clear progression, well-suited to athletes with a defined outdoor season and a true off-season. Criticism: phases are short; previously-built qualities decay during later phases ("eight weeks out from strength when you're peaking on routes").

### 2. Non-linear / undulating (Bechtel — "Logical Progression")

- All energy systems trained year-round; the **weakest one gets the most attention** in any given block.
- "Four burners" analogy: three simmer (maintenance), one boils (focus).
- Within a week, days are differentiated by quality (max strength day / power day / endurance day / climbing day), not blocks of weeks.
- Strength: no de-training, sustainable indefinitely, robust to interruptions like trips.
- Criticism: lower peak ceiling than linear if a true peak is needed.

### 3. Daily Undulating Periodization (Hörst, more recent — DUP)

Hörst's modern update: vary intensity day-to-day rather than week-to-week. Compatible with Bechtel's philosophy. Useful for in-season climbers who want to perform while training.

## Standard phase template (when going linear or hybrid)

For a 12-week block, the common climbing-specific shape is:

| Weeks | Phase | Dominant quality | Climbing | Hangboard |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1–4 | Base / Volume | Hypertrophy, tendon prep, technical mileage | Easy + moderate, high volume | Density hangs / repeaters @ 70–80% MVC |
| 5–8 | Max Strength | Pull max + finger max | Limit bouldering, hard moves | Max hangs 6 × 7s @ 85–95% MVC, weighted pulls |
| 9–11 | Power / Power-Endurance | Convert strength to climbing output | Hard routes, 4×4s, link-ups | Lower volume; one max-hang touch per week |
| 12 | Peak / Deload | Performance | Project days outdoors | Minimal — just spark sessions |

Insert a deload microcycle at the end of each mesocycle (reduced volume + intensity) before moving to the next.

## Microcycle (weekly) structures

A pull-push-rest split that Martin requested maps cleanly:

| Day | Type | Climbing? | Strength? | Mobility/cardio? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | Pull | Climbing (limit/strength) | Weighted pulls + Tindeq fingers | Short mobility |
| Tue | Push / Antagonist | Optional easy climb | Dips, bench, rows | Hip + hamstring |
| Wed | Rest / Mobility | — | — | Mobility long, easy run |
| Thu | Pull | Climbing | Hangboard + pulls | Short mobility |
| Fri | Push / Antagonist | — | Dips, bench, rows | Hip + hamstring |
| Sat | Climb (volume / routes) | Outdoor or gym routes | — | — |
| Sun | Rest | — | — | Long mobility / easy run |

This gives 2 finger-strength stimuli, 2 dedicated antagonist sessions, climbing 3–4×/week, and 2 short runs — fitting the over-40 recovery envelope.

## Concurrent training & interference (running + strength)

Peer-reviewed view: **endurance training at conversational pace, kept short (≤45 min), and on different days from heavy strength**, does not meaningfully interfere with strength adaptations in resistance-trained adults. The interference effect is strongest when high-intensity intervals stack on the same day as strength work.

Implication for Martin: 2–3 easy runs/week on rest or push days are compatible with the pull-up and finger-strength goal. Avoid hill repeats, intervals, or speed work during the max-strength block.

## Over-40 adjustments

- Drop to **2 hard sessions of any given quality per week**; let frequency, not intensity, take the hit.
- **Deload every 3rd or 4th week, not optional.**
- Sleep 7–9 h, protein-bias diet, mobility daily.
- Skill and technique mileage retains value when strength gains slow.
- Recovery between hangboard sessions: 72 h minimum for max-effort fingers.

## Recommended framework for Martin's 12 weeks (Jun 8 – Aug 30, 2026)

**Macro shape: hybrid linear** — 3 × 4-week mesocycles with the boilerplate progression below, with **finger asymmetry work threaded through every phase** (not just one block).

**Phase 1 — Base + Tendon Prep (Weeks 1–4, Jun 8 – Jul 5)**
- Density hangs 75% MVC, both hands separately, weaker side first
- Volume climbing on jugs and crimps below limit
- Weighted pull-up reps in the 5–8 range with 1–2 RIR
- 2 short easy runs, 2 mobility blocks
- Deload week 4

**Phase 2 — Max Strength (Weeks 5–8, Jul 6 – Aug 2)**
- Max hangs 6 × 7s @ 85–90% MVC (Lattice), or heavy isometrics 4–6 × 2–4s @ 80% MVC (Nelson) — alternate sessions
- Weighted pull-ups 3 × 3–5 reps at 85% 1RM, progressing weekly
- Limit bouldering 1×/week
- Continue running easy (cap intensity)
- Deload week 8

**Phase 3 — Power, Performance, Peak (Weeks 9–12, Aug 3 – Aug 30)**
- One max-hang touch session per week (maintenance)
- Power endurance: 4 × 4s, route intervals, hard linkages
- Outdoor projecting on weekends
- Reduced strength volume — refresh, don't add
- Peak / partial deload final week

This framework is the **skeleton**; the actual day-by-day plan with Martin's exact loads gets built once you confirm the structure.
