# Phase 2 — Max Strength (Weeks 5–8)

**Dates:** Mon Jul 6 → Sun Aug 2, 2026.
**Mission:** Convert the tissue capacity built in Phase 1 into measurable strength gains. Drive finger peak force higher with **Lattice-style max hangs** and pull-up 1RM higher with **heavy triples**. Continue closing L-hand asymmetry but with heavier intensities.

This file is the **skeleton.** Exact loads and edges are calibrated from the Week 4 test results before Week 5 begins.

---

## Phase 2 mission, in one paragraph

For 4 weeks, the priority is **maximal force output** on both fingers and pulls. Volume drops, intensity rises, rest extends. Climbing volume reduces — indoor sessions become **limit bouldering**, not lap-running. Outdoor days are mixed: some volume, one or two project attempts. The L hand keeps the priority slot every session but now at near-max intensities.

## Calibration formulas (apply Week 4 test numbers)

After Week 4 retest, new MVC values for R and L drive Phase 2 prescriptions:

- **Max-hang target load (added weight):**
  Two-arm hang on 20 mm half-crimp at 85% MVC.
  Target total load = 0.85 × (R MVC + L MVC) × 2 (since one-arm MVC × 2 ≈ two-arm capacity).
  Added load = target total − bodyweight.

  *Worked example with Phase 1 numbers (R 55, L 52): target = 0.85 × (55+52)×2 ÷ 2 hands = 90.95 kg total per hand × 2 = ~181 kg system load. Two-arm hang: about 0.85 × (55+52) = 91 kg total load (BW + added). At 82 kg BW that's **+9 kg added**. Conservative entry; if Week 4 tests up, target re-computes.*

  > Don't use these numbers — they're an example. Re-run after Week 4 test.

- **Pull-up working load:**
  85% of new estimated 1RM, used for 3–5 rep working sets.

- **Submax repeater target (Thu):**
  75% MVC on Tindeq, one-arm, 7 s pulls × 4–6 reps with 2 min rest between reps. L first, 4 sets each hand.

## Phase 2 non-negotiables

1. **Re-test if anything is off.** If the Week 4 numbers regressed or the L finger feels off, **do not start Phase 2 as written.** Run an extra base week.
2. **No new exercises in Phase 2.** Same lifts, heavier loads. Stability is the point.
3. **Sleep ≥ 7.5 h** before max-hang sessions. Worse sleep → push the session by one day or substitute Thu's lighter protocol.
4. **Climbing intensity follows the gym schedule, not the outdoor schedule.** If Saturday outdoor goes hard, Wednesday limit-bouldering goes light.
5. **Skip ahead to Phase 3 deload if needed.** If the L finger PIP shows up again, Phase 2 ends early and Phase 3 begins at lower intensity.
