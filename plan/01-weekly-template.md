# Weekly Template

The canonical week. Day order and session content stay constant across all 12 weeks; only **loads, hold times, and rep schemes** change per phase. The pull-push-rest split with weekend outdoor priority is locked.

---

## MONDAY — PULL (Heavy)

**Total: ~75 min**

1. **Warm-up (10 min) — Hooper's 3-step**
   - Tendon glides — 10 reps each pattern (fist → hook → straight → table-top → full fist)
   - Band external rotations + scapular retractions — 2 × 12
   - Recruitment pulls on 20 mm — 5 ascending sets at 30/50/70/85/95% effort, 2 min rest

2. **Hangboard — primary finger session** (phase-specific protocol, see Phase docs)
   - **Always L hand first** while fresh
   - Mid-set rest: per protocol
   - Between-set rest: 3–5 min

3. **Weighted pull-ups — primary pull**
   - Pyramid up to working sets, then work
   - Working scheme: phase-specific
   - 3–5 min rest between working sets

4. **Bicep / pulling accessory**
   - Bicep curl 16 kg × 12 reps × 2 sets each arm
   - Hammer curl 9 kg × 12 reps × 2 sets each arm

5. **10-min mobility** — hip 90/90, hamstring active straight-leg, ankle wall test

---

## TUESDAY — PUSH + Antagonist

**Total: ~60 min strength + optional 25–30 min easy run**

1. **Warm-up (5 min)** — band external rotations, scapular wall slides, light dips

2. **Superset A — Vertical** (3 rounds, 3 min rest between rounds)
   - Dips: 5–8 reps @ 1–2 RIR
   - Overhead press: 5–8 reps @ 1–2 RIR (barbell or dumbbell)

3. **Superset B — Horizontal** (3 rounds, 3 min rest between rounds)
   - Barbell row (or seated row): 5–8 reps @ 1–2 RIR
   - Bench press: 5–8 reps @ 1–2 RIR

4. **Antagonist + prehab** (each 2 × 12, no rest between)
   - Lateral raise: 2 kg single-arm × 14
   - Face pulls (band or cable): × 15
   - Reverse fly (light DB): × 12
   - Wrist extensors (light DB curl reverse): × 15

5. **Single-leg knee work** (2 rounds, 2 min rest)
   - Bulgarian split squat: 8 each leg (slow tempo 3-1-3)
   - Step-up (12–18" box): 10 each leg

6. **Hip + hamstring mobility (15 min)** — see exercise library

**Optional easy run (evening):** 25–30 min conversational pace, ≤8:30/km.

---

## WEDNESDAY — INDOOR CLIMBING (Gym)

**Total: 90–120 min including travel**

- Hooper 3-step warm-up at the wall (tendon glides, band ER, recruitment on the easiest holds)
- Easy traverse + technique laps — 15 min
- **Phase-specific focus:**
  - **Phase 1:** Volume — 60+ moves at moderate grade. No projecting.
  - **Phase 2:** Limit bouldering, max-effort attempts on hard problems with full rest. 8–12 attempts total across 60 min.
  - **Phase 3:** Route intervals or 4×4 bouldering depending on emphasis week.
- Cool-down: tendon glides, gentle hangs at 30% effort, 5 min.

**Never project on the left middle finger PIP region until 24 h pain post-session is consistently zero across all of Phase 1.**

---

## THURSDAY — PULL (Asymmetry-focused, lighter)

**Total: ~50 min**

1. **Warm-up (10 min)** — Hooper 3-step + Tindeq recruitment to 50% MVC each hand

2. **Asymmetry-focused finger work** (Tindeq, one-arm)
   - **L hand first, always**, fully rested
   - 4 sets of phase-specific protocol
   - Match volume on R hand at the same **relative % MVC**, not the same absolute kg

3. **Weighted pull-ups — secondary**
   - Lighter than Monday: phase-specific (typically Mon load minus 5–10 kg)
   - 3 sets × 5–8 reps @ 2–3 RIR

4. **Core**
   - Hollow hold 3 × 30s
   - Pallof press (band) 2 × 12 each side
   - Hanging leg raise 3 × 8–10

5. **10-min mobility** — same as Monday

---

## FRIDAY — REST + Long Mobility + Easy Run

**Total: 30 min mobility + 30–40 min easy run**

1. **Long mobility flow (30 min)** — see exercise library, full sequence:
   - Hip openers: 90/90, pigeon (L + R), couch stretch
   - Hamstring: Jefferson curl bodyweight only (3 × 8), supine PNF
   - Ankle: wall test, calf rocks, banded distraction
   - Deep-squat hold (assisted with rack/wall, then bodyweight) — accumulate 2 min total
   - T-spine: cat-cow, thread-the-needle, foam roll
   - Shoulder: stick dislocates × 30

2. **Easy run** — 30–40 min, conversational, ≤8:30/km. **No intervals, no hills, no pace work.** If knee feels off, cut to 20 min or skip.

---

## SATURDAY — OUTDOOR CLIMBING

**Phase-specific intent:**
- **Phase 1 (weeks 1–3):** Volume + technique. Climb routes at or below your onsight grade. **No projecting at limit.** Goal is mileage, movement quality, and re-introducing climbing-specific load on tendons after structured training Mon–Thu.
- **Phase 1 Week 4:** Reduced volume — only 1–2 hard pitches, lots of jugs.
- **Phase 2 (weeks 5–7):** Mix — some volume routes + 1–2 hard attempts on a project.
- **Phase 2 Week 8:** Easy onsight only.
- **Phase 3 (weeks 9–11):** Project days. Hard attempts, full rest between, 4–6 burns max.
- **Phase 3 Week 12:** Performance — full-go on best project.

**Always:** Hooper 3-step warm-up before pulling on rock. Cool-down stretching at the crag, not after the drive home.

---

## SUNDAY — REST or 2nd Outdoor Day

**Default: Full rest** with optional 30-min mobility walk.

**Allowed exception:** A second outdoor day if (a) Saturday was sub-limit, (b) no finger discomfort, and (c) Sunday will also be sub-limit. Two limit-effort outdoor days back-to-back are off the table for the full 12 weeks.

## Non-negotiables (in priority order)

1. **24-hour pain rule on the left middle finger.** If next-morning soreness exceeds mild stiffness, the previous session was too much — drop one set or 5% intensity for the next session.
2. **Warm-up before every finger session, no skipping.** Hooper 3-step, ~10 min.
3. **L hand first** on every finger session, every session, every phase.
4. **Outdoor climbing days are sacred.** The plan flexes around them, not the other way around.
5. **Sleep ≥ 7 h** before any Monday or Thursday session, or drop to recovery-day mode.
6. **Skip a session before climbing fatigued.** Better to lose a session than to grind through a re-injury.
