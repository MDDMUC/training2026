## WEEK 1 — Onboarding (Jun 8 – Jun 14)

The single most important goal of Week 1 is **not getting injured** while re-entering structure. Loads are deliberately conservative; the L middle finger gets the easiest re-entry.

### Mon Jun 8 — PULL (Heavy)

**Hangboard — Density Hangs, 25 mm edge, half-crimp + open-hand alternating sets**

L hand first. Tindeq target: **39 kg (75% MVC)** for L, **41 kg** for R.

| Set | Reps | Hold | Rest within set | Hand |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2 | 20 s | 15 s | L (half-crimp) |
| 2 | 2 | 20 s | 15 s | R (half-crimp) |
| 3 | 2 | 20 s | 15 s | L (open-hand) |
| 4 | 2 | 20 s | 15 s | R (open-hand) |

3 min rest between sets. **24-hour pain rule applies — if the left PIP feels even mildly aggravated tomorrow, drop to 3 sets next session.**

**Weighted pull-ups — pyramid + work**

**Accessories**

**Mobility 10 min** — hip 90/90, hamstring active, ankle wall test.

### Tue Jun 9 — PUSH + Antagonist

**Hip + hamstring mobility 15 min**

**Optional run:** 25 min easy, ≤8:30/km, in the evening if energy is good.

## WEEK 2 — Volume Build (Jun 15 – Jun 21)

Same structure. Two changes:
1. **Density Hangs:** add one set per session (5 total instead of 4) and extend holds to 25 s.
2. **Weighted pulls:** working load up to **+20 kg × 6 reps × 3 sets**.

### Mon Jun 15 — PULL (Heavy)

Hangboard, 5 sets × 2 reps × 25 s @ 75% MVC, L-first alternating half-crimp / open-hand.

Pull-up working sets: +20 kg × 6 × 3 sets after the same pyramid warm-up.

Everything else identical to Mon Jun 8.

### Tue Jun 16 — PUSH

Bump working loads up ~2 kg per lift if Week 1 felt easy (e.g., dips +2 kg added, bench 57.5 kg, row 52.5 kg, press 32.5 kg). RIR stays at 2.

### Wed Jun 17 — INDOOR CLIMB

Volume — 80–100 moves, same grade range. Add one harder boulder problem (one grade above comfort) **only if shoulders and fingers feel fresh.**

### Thu Jun 18 — PULL (Asymmetry)

Same as Thu Jun 11. Add one set per hand (5 each instead of 4).

### Fri Jun 19 — REST + Mob + Run

Run extended to 35 min if pain-free.

### Sat Jun 20 — OUTDOOR

5–7 routes. Can include one route at onsight level (not above).

### Sun Jun 21 — REST or optional 2nd outdoor

Allowed: a second day if Saturday was sub-limit and Sunday will also be. Capped at 4 routes Sunday.

## WEEK 3 — Peak Volume of Phase 1 (Jun 22 – Jun 28)

Highest volume week of the phase. After this, deload.

Changes:
1. **Density Hangs:** 6 sets × 2 reps × 30 s @ 75% MVC. Drop to 20 mm edge (from 25 mm) for half-crimp sets.
2. **Weighted pulls:** working load **+22 kg × 6 × 3 sets** + one set of **+18 kg × 8 reps** at the end.
3. **Thursday asymmetry:** 5 sets each hand × 7 s holds (up from 6 s).

### Mon Jun 22, Tue Jun 23, Wed Jun 24, Thu Jun 25, Fri Jun 26

Same structure as Week 2 with the increments above.

### Sat Jun 27 — OUTDOOR

Permission to attempt one project pitch at **one grade above onsight**. **Not at limit.** Try, then leave it.

### Sun Jun 28 — REST

Full rest. No optional second day this weekend — Week 4 deload starts Monday.

## WEEK 4 — Deload + Retest (Jun 29 – Jul 5)

Reduce volume by ~40% and intensity by ~10–15%. End the week with the test session that resets numbers for Phase 2.

### Mon Jun 29 — PULL (Light)

Hangboard: **3 sets × 2 × 20 s @ 70% MVC**, L-first. That's it.

Weighted pulls: 3 × 5 @ +15 kg. No backoffs, no accessories.

10 min mobility. Out in 35 min.

### Tue Jun 30 — PUSH (Light)

Single round of each superset at Week 1 loads. No antagonist superset. Knee single-leg only — 1 set per side. Mobility 15 min.

### Wed Jul 1 — INDOOR CLIMB (Easy)

40–60 moves, jugs only. Technique focus. **No projecting whatsoever.**

### Thu Jul 2 — REST

Full rest. 20 min mobility only.

### Fri Jul 3 — REST

Mobility 30 min. Optional easy 20-min walk. No run.

### Sat Jul 4 — TEST SESSION

Full testing protocol (see `05-testing-protocol.md`). Approximately 60 min including warm-up.

1. **Tindeq peak force** — both hands, 20 mm half-crimp, one-arm, 3 reps per hand with 2 min rest.
2. **Weighted pull-up estimated 1RM** — work up to a heavy triple, calculate 1RM.
3. Optional: Critical Force test on Tindeq (only if motivation and energy are high).
4. Log everything. Compute asymmetry %.

### Sun Jul 5 — Reset

Full rest. Review Phase 1 metrics together with Claude, draft Phase 2 detailed loads from the new test numbers.

## Things that should be true by end of Phase 1

- L-hand peak force has gained 1–3 kg; asymmetry has narrowed.
- Pull-up 1RM has gained 1–3 kg.
- 30 min easy run feels routine.
- Deep-squat assisted hold is comfortable for 60 s.
- No lingering left middle finger PIP sensation in the morning.

If any of these are conspicuously off, **we adjust Phase 2 before starting it**, not after.
