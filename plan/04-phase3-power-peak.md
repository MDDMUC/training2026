# Phase 3 — Power & Peak (Weeks 9–12)

**Dates:** Mon Aug 3 → Sun Aug 30, 2026.
**Mission:** Convert raw strength into climbing-specific output, then maintain through a performance window. This is the **express the strength** phase, not the build-it phase. Training volume drops sharply; climbing intensity rises.

This file is the **skeleton.** Calibrated after Week 8 retest.

---

## Mission

Phase 1 built tissue. Phase 2 built strength. Phase 3 makes that strength **available on rock** through:
- Power-endurance: route intervals, 4×4 bouldering, hard linkups
- Maintenance hangboard: 1 max-hang touch per week, no more
- Climbing-as-primary-stimulus: indoor session prioritised for performance work
- Reduced strength volume: enough to maintain, not enough to fatigue
- A genuine performance attempt outdoors on the last weekend

Since the August peak is **general performance** (not a specific trip), Phase 3 is more relaxed than a hard-peak taper. It's about staying sharp.

## Phase 3 protocols (templates)

### Wednesday indoor — choose ONE per week

**Option A: Route intervals (preferred if lead access feels good)**
- Warm-up: 2 easy routes (6a / 6b)
- 3 routes at 7a–7b+ flash level, with 8–10 min rest between
- 1 route at on-sight maximum
- Cool-down: 1 easy lap

**Option B: 4×4 bouldering (when routes feel stale)**
- 4 boulder problems at V3–V5 (sub-limit)
- Climb all 4 in sequence with no rest (~3–5 min total)
- Rest 4 min
- Repeat 3 more times (4 rounds total)

**Option C: Limit bouldering + power-endurance hybrid**
- 60 min limit attempts on 3 hard problems, full rest
- Finish with 1 round of 4×4 at moderate grade

### Outdoor Saturday — project structure

- Hooper 3-step warm-up at the base
- 1 easy lead (6a–6b) to warm rock
- **4–6 attempts on project**, full rest (15+ min) between
- 1 cool-down lap on something fun

Stop after 6 burns regardless of progress. The body remembers patterns better with fresh efforts than ground-out fatigue.

## Week 12 specifics — Taper

- Mon Aug 24: One light hangboard set per hand (75% MVC × 7 s), 2 light pull-up sets at 65% 1RM. 30 min max session.
- Tue Aug 25: Mobility only. No push session.
- Wed Aug 26: **Final retest** — Tindeq + pull-up 1RM + Critical Force.
- Thu Aug 27: Rest. Easy walk only.
- Fri Aug 28: Rest. Long mobility.
- Sat Aug 29: **Performance day** outdoor — best project, fully rested.
- Sun Aug 30: Rest. Phase 3 review.

---

## Phase 3 non-negotiables

1. **Climbing performance is the primary signal.** Training quality is measured by how it feels on rock, not by gym numbers.
2. **No new strength PR attempts after Week 11.** The pull-up 1RM test happens once at the end and that's it.
3. **Rest more than feels comfortable.** Under-recovery undoes a peak more reliably than under-training.
4. **The L hand is no longer flagged.** By Phase 3, the asymmetry should be under 3%. If it isn't, Phase 3 includes one extra Thu submax session per week with the L hand, no R-hand matching.
