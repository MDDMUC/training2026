# Exercise Library

Form notes, prescription defaults, and what each exercise is *actually* for. Reference this when a session calls out a name without restating the form.

## Finger / Forearm

### Density Hangs (Nelson)

- **Setup:** Half-crimp or open-hand on 20–30 mm wood edge. Body weight only (no added load) — Tindeq reads force, not the bar.
- **Execution:** Sit under the bar, pull up to a target force of ~75% MVC, hold 20–40 s, release for 10–20 s, repeat for 2–3 reps to muscular failure. The hold is **active** — you're producing force the whole time, not passively dangling.
- **What it trains:** Muscle-tendon junction density and stiffness; tendon collagen turnover.
- **Why before max hangs:** Lower joint stress, no peak-load risk on the pulley.

### Max Hangs (Lattice)

- **Setup:** Two-arm hang on 20 mm wood edge, half-crimp.
- **Execution:** 6 reps × 7 s @ 85–90% MVC (added load to push BW + added near 85% of best 7-s max), 2–3 min rest between reps.
- **What it trains:** Maximal recruitable force in the climbing-specific position.
- **Critical:** Warm up to the load with at least 3 ascending sets. Never start cold.

### Submax Repeaters (Nelson, asymmetry day)

- **Setup:** Tindeq, one-arm, 20 mm half-crimp.
- **Execution:** 4 sets × 7 s pulls @ 75% MVC, 90 s rest. L first, then R.
- **What it trains:** Asymmetry-targeted volume and tendon stimulus without max-effort stress.

### Recruitment Pulls (Hooper warm-up)

- **Setup:** 20 mm edge or your home hangboard's good edge, two-arm.
- **Execution:** 5 ascending sets, 3 s pulls at 30 / 50 / 70 / 85 / 95% effort. 2 min rest between sets.
- **What it does:** Wakes up neural drive and primes pulleys without inducing fatigue.

### Tendon Glides

- **Execution:** Slowly cycle the fingers through 5 positions — straight → hook → table-top → straight fist → full fist. 10 reps each direction. Both hands simultaneously.
- **What it does:** Glides the FDP and FDS tendons through the pulley system, lubricates and warms the flexor sheath.

## Pull / Upper-back

### Weighted Pull-ups

- **Setup:** Full overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulders. Belt with plates.
- **Execution:** Dead hang start, pull until chin clears the bar, controlled negative to full extension. **No kipping.** 3 s up, 1 s top, 3 s down for working reps. Strict.
- **Rep schemes:**
  - Phase 1: 3–4 × 5–8 @ 70% 1RM, 1–2 RIR
  - Phase 2: 3–4 × 3–5 @ 85% 1RM, 1 RIR
  - Phase 3: 3 × 2–3 @ 90% 1RM, 0–1 RIR

### Barbell Row (or Seated Cable Row)

- **Setup:** Hinged hip, neutral spine, bar to lower sternum.
- **Execution:** 5–8 reps @ 1–2 RIR. Pull elbows back, squeeze scaps at top.

### Bicep Curl (16 kg dumbbell)

- 12 reps × 2 sets per arm. Slow eccentric (3 s down).

### Hammer Curl (9 kg dumbbell)

- 12 reps × 2 sets per arm. Brachialis and brachioradialis focus.

## Push / Antagonist

### Dips

- **Setup:** Parallel bars, body upright (not bent forward — that's chest-focused; we want triceps and shoulder balance).
- **Execution:** 5–8 reps @ 1–2 RIR. Add weight via belt once bodyweight is easy for 8.

### Overhead Press

- **Setup:** Standing barbell or DB, neutral spine, glutes engaged.
- **Execution:** 5–8 reps @ 1–2 RIR. Press straight up, full lockout.

### Bench Press

- **Setup:** Flat bench, shoulder-width grip, feet planted.
- **Execution:** 5–8 reps @ 1–2 RIR. Bar to chest, controlled.

### Lateral Raise (single-arm, 2 kg)

- 14 reps × 2 sets per arm. Slow, controlled, no swing. Lateral deltoid focus.

### Face Pulls (band or rope cable)

- 15 reps × 2 sets. Pull to forehead, elbows high. Rear-delt + external rotation.

### Reverse Fly (light DB)

- 12 reps × 2 sets. Hinged hip, arms wide, squeeze rear delts.

### Wrist Extensors (light DB reverse curl)

- 15 reps × 2 sets. Bar resting on fingertips, raise via wrist extension only.

### Wrist curls — Forearm balance (both directions)

**Why both directions:** climbers have a 6.27:1 flexor-to-extensor ratio on average (vs the population's 3.72:1). That imbalance is the leading cause of medial/lateral elbow tendinopathy in climbing. Training extensors brings the ratio toward 3:1 over time.

**Setup:** seated, forearm resting on thigh or bench so only the wrist moves. Slow tempo (3 s up · 1 s pause · 3 s down). Stop ≥ 2 reps in reserve — these are prehab, not max work.

**Wrist extension** (palm-down / knuckles-up curl)
- The priority direction for climbers.
- Light DB (~5 kg starting). Palm faces the floor, knuckles point up at the top.
- 15 reps × 4 sets (alternating L/R, 2 per side). 60 s rest.
- The under-trained climbing antagonist.

**Wrist flexion** (palm-up curl)
- Secondary direction. Climbing already overworks the flexors via grip.
- Heavier DB (~9 kg starting — flexors are stronger).
- 15 reps × 4 sets (alternating L/R, 2 per side). 60 s rest.
- Trains end-range flexion the way grip alone doesn't.

**Frequency:** prescribed on **Tue** (push/antagonist day) and **Thu** (pull-light day) — 2× per week. Small muscle groups tolerate higher frequency than large ones. Skipped in deload weeks (Week 4 of each phase).

**Not on Mon/Sat:** Mon is heavy-pull day with bicep + hammer curls already loading the forearms; adding wrist work would over-train flexors. Sat is outdoor climbing — let the climbing do the work.

### "No Moneys" (band external rotation)

- 8–12 reps × 2–3 sets. Band around forearms, elbows at sides, externally rotate hands apart while keeping elbows pinned. Hooper's go-to.

### Stick Dislocates

- 30–60 s × 2–3 sets. PVC pipe or broomstick wide grip, pass over head front to back. Shoulder mobility + warm-up.

## Core

### Hollow Hold

- 30 s × 3 sets. Low back pressed to floor, legs straight + raised, arms overhead.

### Pallof Press (band)

- 12 each side × 2 sets. Anti-rotation. Band perpendicular to body, press out and hold.

### Hanging Leg Raise

- 8–10 × 3 sets. Hang from bar, raise straight legs to 90° or higher. Controlled.

## Lower body / Knee work

### Bulgarian Split Squat

- **Setup:** Rear foot elevated on bench, front foot flat, torso upright.
- **Execution:** 8 reps each leg @ 3-1-3 tempo (3 s down, 1 s pause, 3 s up). BW initially, add load once BW × 10 feels easy.
- **Knee focus:** front knee tracks over middle toe; depth as deep as the surgical knee comfortably allows.

### Step-up (12–18" box)

- **Execution:** 10 reps each leg, full hip extension at top. No push-off from the trail leg.

### Deep-Squat Hold (assisted progression)

1. **Week 1–2:** Hold rack/wall with both hands, squat as deep as comfortable, hold 30 s × 4.
2. **Week 3–4:** One hand on support, 30 s × 4.
3. **Week 5–6:** Bodyweight only, no support, 30 s × 3.
4. **Week 7+:** Loaded goblet hold (light kettlebell), 20 s × 3.

Goal: 60 s unsupported BW deep squat by Week 8.

## Lattice-grounded deep-squat / knee mobility routine

Sourced from Lattice Training's flexibility library — specifically the side-split, pancake, and conditioning-mobility blog content. Threaded into **Tuesday** (push day, after strength) as a dedicated 15-min block, and re-emphasized on **Friday** during the long mobility flow. Two exposures per week; matches Lattice's "1–2× per week alongside daily lower-intensity mobility" guidance.

**Why these specific drills for Martin:** the surgical right knee + the stated deep-squat goal + the over-40 mobility focus all converge on hip-adductor + knee-tracking work. The four anchor drills below cover all three.

### Cossack Squats — knee-mobility primary

- **Targets:** hip adductors, hip flexion, knee tracking through deep range
- **Sets × reps:** 3 × 4 reps
- **Tempo:** 3 s down · 2 s pause at bottom · 2 s up · 1 s pause at top (Lattice spec)
- **Rest:** 60 s between sets
- **Form:** Wide stance, one knee bends as you shift weight to that side, opposite leg extends straight. **Knee must not travel beyond the foot** — non-negotiable for the surgical knee. Hands forward for counterbalance. Slow throughout — moving fast triggers muscle reflexes that limit range.
- **Progression:** start bodyweight, hold a 5 kg plate at chest after 4–6 weeks consistent.

### Horse Stance — adductors at end-range

- **Targets:** hip adductors (strengthening), wide-stance squat depth
- **Sets × reps:** 3 × 30 s isometric hold (Lattice spec)
- **Rest:** 45 s between sets
- **Form:** Stand with feet together → externally rotate both feet (heels together) → take weight to balls of feet → internally rotate, walking feet wider, until you're wider than shoulder-width with toes pointing out. Squat down to where you can hold. **Knees track over toes, never cave in.** Maintain the same height for the full hold — no bouncing.
- **Tension build:** ramp slowly across the first 20–30 s, deepen every 10–15 s if possible to stay at maximum tolerable limit.
- **Progression:** add a light plate held at chest after 4–6 weeks at bodyweight.

### Half-Split ISO — per side

- **Targets:** hip adductors + hamstrings at length, hip mobility per side
- **Sets × reps:** 3 × 30 s per side (alternating L/R, so 6 sets total in the prescription)
- **Rest:** 30 s between sides
- **Form:** Front leg in a deep half-split, rear leg extended laterally. Place your pelvis into an **anterior pelvic tilt** and maintain it throughout. If hands have to touch the floor for balance, use yoga blocks.

### Deep-Squat Assisted Hold — goal-specific

- **Targets:** Martin's stated H2 2026 goal — unsupported BW deep squat for 60 s.
- **Sets × reps:** 2 × 60 s (one assisted + one unsupported attempt if range allows)
- **Rest:** 30 s
- **Progression mirrors the existing 4-week ladder** above. By end of Phase 1 (Sun Jul 5), target = 60 s unsupported.

### Frequency

| Day | Block | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Tue (Push) | Dedicated Lattice routine — all 4 drills | ~12–15 min |
| Fri (Long Mobility) | Cossack + Horse + Deep-squat hold threaded into the broader 30-min flow | ~6 min within the flow |

Deload weeks (4/8/12) keep the Friday touches but Tuesday's dedicated routine still runs (mobility doesn't tank recovery; it aids it).

## Mobility — daily flows

### Short flow (10 min, Mon/Thu)

- 90/90 hip switch — 1 min each side
- Active straight-leg hamstring raise — 10 each leg
- Ankle wall test + calf rock — 1 min each ankle
- Cat-cow / thoracic flow — 2 min

### Long flow (30 min, Fri / Sun)

- Foam roll calves, quads, IT band, glutes — 8 min
- Pigeon — 2 min each side
- Couch stretch (rear-leg quad/hip flexor on bench) — 2 min each side
- Jefferson curl BW only — 3 × 8 (slow flexion–extension)
- Supine PNF hamstring with band — 5 reps each side
- Ankle band distraction — 2 min each
- Deep-squat hold — 2 min total accumulated
- T-spine: thread-the-needle, cat-cow, prone Y/T/W — 5 min
- Stick dislocates — 30 reps

## Running

### Easy run

- **Pace:** conversational, current ~8:30/km, never harder. Heart rate Zone 2 (130–150 bpm depending on the day).
- **Duration:** Phase 1 30–40 min, Phase 2 25–30 min, Phase 3 25 min (1× per week), Week 12 off.
- **Surface:** soft if possible (grass, trail, packed dirt) for the surgical knee.
- **Footwear:** cushioned daily trainers, not race-day flats.
- **Cadence target:** 170+ spm; if cadence drops, you're heel-striking or fatigued — slow down.

**Stop and walk** if:
- Knee clicks, catches, or hurts (not just "tightness")
- Pace drops > 1 min/km below baseline
- HR climbs above 165 at conversational pace

## Climbing — defined intensities

### Volume climbing (Phase 1 indoor)
- Grades 1–2 below onsight, 60–100 moves total, no projecting.

### Limit bouldering (Phase 2 indoor)
- Hardest problems available, max-effort tries, 3–5 min rest between attempts, 8–12 attempts total.

### Route intervals (Phase 3 indoor)
- 3 lead routes at flash-grade with 8–10 min rest each.

### 4×4 bouldering (Phase 3 indoor)
- 4 problems × 4 rounds, no rest within round, 4 min between rounds.

### Project day (Phase 3 outdoor)
- 4–6 attempts on a goal route/boulder, 15+ min rest between, full effort each go.

### Onsight / sub-limit outdoor
- Routes at or below current onsight grade, focus on movement quality.
